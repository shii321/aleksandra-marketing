FAQ

Sometimes everything is done right, and it still doesn't work. What then?

Often at the start, the tool hasn't been tested yet. The question is — how much time and budget are you willing to invest in testing? And does it even make sense if you haven't gotten there before? After all, clients were still finding you somehow. It's always better to put your money where the sales are guaranteed. Keep a separate budget for tests. And the starting goal is to attract at least one qualified lead from that channel who actually buys. That's it. If there's one sale — there will be a second.

When is it time to stop and admit that the hypothesis just didn't take off?

And where did that hypothesis come from in the first place? If it came from a competitor — let's look at what exactly they did and what you missed. Copying without analysis is a losing lottery. I only build hypotheses from numbers: sales, clicks, views, conversions. We never test the same hypothesis twice without making fundamental changes. We always change the approach. You can stop as early as the second or third run if results are near zero.
